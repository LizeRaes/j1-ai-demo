Data Privacy and User Data Handling - Internal Policy Summary

Purpose and scope:
This policy defines how MedicalAppointment collects, uses, stores, and protects user data in support of operational workflows across scheduling, billing, and customer support.
It applies to all internal teams that interact with user-facing systems, including dispatching, billing, engineering, and support operations.
All personnel must follow least-privilege access and only process data needed for a documented business purpose.

Data minimization principles:
Only required fields should be collected at intake and during support processing.
Temporary troubleshooting artifacts must be kept to the minimum practical detail and removed when no longer needed.
Logs and exported reports must avoid unnecessary personal details unless explicitly required for incident response.

MedicalAppointment processes user data strictly for the purpose of providing appointment scheduling, billing, and account support services.

Stored data includes user identifiers, appointment details, payment status indicators, and basic account information. MedicalAppointment does not store medical records or sensitive clinical data.

Payment credentials are not stored by MedicalAppointment. Any payment-related interactions are handled through approved payment system flows.

User data access is restricted based on role and operational necessity. Support agents may only access information required to resolve a reported issue.

Any concern related to unauthorized data access, exposure, or misuse must be escalated according to the Security Escalation Policy.

Operational controls:
Access to user data should be authenticated, attributable, and reviewable through system logs.
Sensitive support actions should be traceable to a user, timestamp, and ticket context where possible.
When sharing troubleshooting context across teams, redact non-essential identifying fields.
Copying production data into ad-hoc local files is prohibited unless explicitly approved for incident handling.

Retention and deletion guidance:
User data should be retained only as long as required by service delivery, legal obligations, or incident analysis needs.
Obsolete support artifacts and duplicate exports should be deleted promptly.
If a dataset is generated solely for short-term debugging, include an expiration note and cleanup owner.

Incident and escalation expectations:
If a team member suspects unauthorized access, data leakage, or policy misuse, escalate immediately and preserve relevant evidence.
Do not continue broad data inspection after suspicion is raised; switch to controlled incident-response workflow.
Follow the Security Escalation Policy for severity classification, notification, and documented follow-up actions.

Compliance note:
This summary is an internal operational guideline and should be interpreted together with service-level security controls and team runbooks.
